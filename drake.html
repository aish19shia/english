SIR FRANCIS DRAKE

Francis Drake's father was a minister for the navy and Francis grew up near the sea. He went to work for a sea captain at a young age and found his home living and working on a ship.

His first expedition was with John Hawkins. He captained the ship Judith , one of six ships that made up the fleet. Hawkins first led the fleet to Africa then they sailed across the Atlantic to the New World.

Ship Judith

Drake and his fleet of five ships left England on November 15, 1577. He captained the largest ship, the Pelican, which he would later rename the Golden Hind.


Golden Hind

After three years of sailing, on September 26, 1580, the ship arrived back home in England. Drake's expedition was only the second in history to sail around the world. Queen Elizabeth knighted him and he was now known as Sir Francis Drake.

TIMELINE

  • 1540:
    Francis Drake was born in Tavistock, Devon.

  • 1549:
    The Drake family move to Kent because Devon is not safe for Protestants.

  • 1553:
    Francis Drake starts to work on a ship.

  • 1558:
    Elizabeth I becomes Queen of England.

  • 1560:
    Francis Drake becomes a captain.

  • 1567-72:
    Voyages to Africa, America and the West Indies.

  • 1577-80
    Francis Drake sails around the world.

  • 1581-85
    Drake goes into politics: He becomes Mayor of Plymouth and a Member of Parliament for Plymouth.

  • 1587:
    Drake attacks the Spanish port Cadiz and destroys many Spanish ships.

  • 1588:
    Drake is Vice Admiral of the fleet that destroys the Spanish Armada.

  • 1596:
    Francis Drake dies on his ship in the Carribbean.
